Featured Automation Projects

Autonomous UVC Disinfection Robot

The Challenge: Develop a fully autonomous robotic system for pathogen disinfection in critical environments during the pandemic, requiring a solution that was both effective and safe for remote operation.

My Contribution: As development leader, I owned the project from concept to commissioning. I personally designed the complete mechatronic system in SolidWorks and created realistic simulations in 3DS MAX. A key part of my hands-on contribution was integrating the Omron AMR platform with a custom sensor array (LIDAR, vision) and programming the initial safety and operational firmware on an Arduino. I also managed the project's most critical technical constraint: balancing the significant weight of the power sources against the UVC emitters' high energy demands, all while ensuring the safety interlocks were fail-proof.

UV Curing System for Metal Printing (CMP)

The Challenge: Create a high-performance UV curing system for an industrial metal printing line that required precise, real-time control over multiple physical parameters, including temperature, hydraulic flow, and electrical load.

My Contribution: My role was to architect and build the entire electromechanical control system from the ground up. I executed the full mechanical design in SolidWorks and the electrical schematics in EPLAN. I then personally programmed the Siemens PLC and HMI from scratch, developing a robust control logic that included custom PID loops for precise LED temperature management, hydraulic flow control for the cooling system, and variable current control for the panel fans—all while ensuring the final system was fully compliant with NR-12 safety standards.

Compact UV Curing System for Offset Press (Astergraf)

The Challenge: Integrate a powerful UV curing system into a high-speed offset printing press, a task made incredibly difficult by an extremely constrained physical space and the requirement to operate within a 2mm tolerance of fast-moving machine components.

My Contribution: Faced with the reality that no off-the-shelf components would work, my contribution was to engineer a solution from first principles. I used 3D printing to rapidly prototype and test several iterations of a custom, ultra-compact, water-cooled LED module housing. I also designed and fabricated bespoke hydraulic connections to fit the tight space. This process of hands-on iteration and custom fabrication was the key to delivering a functional system that solved a critical production bottleneck for the client.

Arduino-Based UV Curing Conveyor

The Challenge: A client required a cost-effective, automated conveyor system for a new production process involving instant adhesives. The solution needed precise UV radiation exposure control and integrated operator safety features, without the budget for a full industrial PLC system.

My Contribution: I developed this project as a complete turnkey solution, owning the process from concept to commissioning. My hands-on work covered every aspect: I designed the conveyor's mechanical structure in SolidWorks, ensuring stability for delicate products; selected all hardware components; and built the control system using an Arduino integrated with a touch screen. I personally programmed the control logic and the interactive HMI, assembled and wired the electronics, and commissioned the full system at the client's facility.
